How To:
Origami Succulent

Introduction

The following instructions will take you step-by-step through the making of an origami paper succulent. It will take about 15 minutes to complete. Origami is a practice that is best served by crisp, clean folds, so pick up a piece of paper and follow along!

Tools and Supplies

The overall cost will be about $6.

  • Scissors

    A sharp pair of scissors to make clean cuts.

  • Origami Paper

    2 pieces of 15 cm x 15 cm, double-sided, green origami paper.

  • Clay Pot

    A 3-5‚Äù pot is the perfect new home for your complete succulent!

  • Starburst Flower (optional)

    Add to show your succulent in bloom! You can find the tutorial here.

  • Crinkle Paper

    Crinkle paper becomes the soil for your potted succulent.

  • Rubber Bands

    Secures your succulent leaves together.

Step 1 Cut the square in half diagonally.

Lay the paper in front of you like a diamond and fold diagonally, corner to corner. Cut the paper in half down the center to make two triangles. Keep one triangle, and put the other one aside.

Step 2 Fold triangle shape in half.

Orient the triangle with the long side along the bottom. Fold in half to make a smaller, right angle triangle. Pinch and run fingers along the recently folded edge to enforce the fold.

Step 3 Create a square.

Unfold your right angle triangle and orient with the point facing up and the long side on the bottom. Fold the bottom right corner up to the top point with the edge lining up with the center fold. Repeat on the other side. This will leave you with a diamond.

Step 4 Create a kite.

Orient the diamond with the folded edges on the bottom and the open folds pointing up. Fold down the right corner of the diamond and align the open edge with the center fold. Repeat on the other side. This will leave you with a kite shape.

Step 5 Create a triangle.

Fold up the bottom corner of the kite along the horizontal line to make your kite into a triangle. Release this fold so that it leaves a crease and returns to the kite shape.

Step 6 Invert the folds to create two triangle flaps.

Unfold the right half of the triangle and invert the fold. Pinch and crease to along the edge created in step 4. This will create a triangle shaped flap. Repeat on both sides.

Step 7 Create a crease to divide the flaps in half.

Fold down the right triangle flap so that the open side of the fold is aligned with the horizontal crease from the bottom triangle. Repeat this on the other side, and then unfold to leave the triangular flaps pointing upwards.

Step 8 Finish the leaf.

Fold up the bottom triangle along the horizontal edge so that the corner points up. You have now completed one leaf of the succulent! Repeat these steps three more times to create a total of four leaves.

Step 9 Create a leaf line up.

With your four succulent leaves, line up the bottom folded edges. Use the bottom triangle folds to secure the leaves in a row, overlapping them over and under.

Step 10 Secure the leaf line by folding.

Once all of the leaves are aligned and interlocking via overlap, fold the new bottom edge of the leaf line up and over to match the crease at the bottom of the flaps. This new fold is meant to secure the leaves together as one unit.

Step 11 Roll the leaf line so that both ends meet.

Use your finger to help roll the line of leaves until both ends meet. Once they meet, interlock them together by sliding one end into the other. Make sure they are nestled tightly together.

Step 12 Secure the leaves with a rubber band.

Once the leaves are rolled and interlocking, wrap a rubber band around the base of the succulent in order to make sure the leaves are secure.

Step 13 Shape your succulent.

Use your finger to expand and shape the leaves of the succulent. Bend the leaves outward from the base and use your finger or a pen to shape them.

Complete Pot your succulent.

Grab a pot and some crinkle paper and plant your succulent, making sure to cover up the base and rubber band. For an added touch, spruce up your succulent by placing an origami starburst flower in the center.
